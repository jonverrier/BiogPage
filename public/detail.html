Amateur offroad motorcycling feels relief after a somewhat botched water crossing.

Practicing riding through slop in a fallow field.

Terrain - muddy.

The 80 mile loop around West Dorset.

My heart rate tracker for the day - mostly up around 100.

Smear and roaming in West Dorset.

We were somewhere around Cerne Abbas on the edge of Dorset when the Full English began to take hold.

Unusually for a Rotor adventure, this one had started at the New Inn, with a much needed infusion of eggs, bacon, and coffee. 8.45 for breakfast, wheels rolling at a leisurely 10.

It was mid January, and rainy, with standing water and slop all around the trails. We headed out North, and then West, a big loop via Beaminster and Bridport. The water crossings were pretty deep, and I made sure to test this out by stalling in one.

Stan had taken us training the previous day, trying to get us to expend less effort on the bikes when in slop, making small adjustments to riding position by anticipating throttle roll on with a small lean forward, and the opposite on the brakes. And plenty of practice in the slop - roll off before you hit it, then gentle drive through with weight a little back. Or a lot back if it’s deep . This all helped out on the day.

We did about 80 miles. Less than usual, but Winter hours meant early dusk, and the water meant mud everywhere which slowed progress. We had the classic Rotor Lunch, on one of Stan’s ‘Forecourts of the West Coast’ after refuelling.

Heart rate was mostly up about 100 when we were riding, off a resting rate less then 40, which shows the effort of managing the bike through the slop. And also shows the value of Stan’s training trying to get us to be more economical with the Body English.

The Bikes performed immaculately, reflecting the quality of the Yamaha engineering, and the Crescent and Rotor care that goes into them.
